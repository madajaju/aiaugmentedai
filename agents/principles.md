# AI-Augmented Website Guiding Principles

Version 1.1

## Purpose

The AI-Augmented website exists to advance the AI-Augmented movement.

The site is the public front door for the movement.

The site is not primarily a book website, consulting website, training website, podcast website, or personal brand website.

The site exists to help people understand why becoming AI-Augmented matters, choose the lens that fits them, discover where they are on their journey, and take the next step toward becoming AI-Augmented.

Books, assessments, workshops, courses, podcasts, tools, and consulting are supporting mechanisms that help people progress on that journey.

---

# Movement Perspective

The movement is the organizing lens for the entire site.

The site should make the movement feel:

* Clear
* Credible
* Practical
* Human-centered
* Worth joining

The movement is not a slogan.

It is the public expression of a shared operating standard for becoming AI-Augmented.

The movement page should explain:

* Why the movement exists
* Who it is for
* What it means to become AI-Augmented
* How the public launch is sequenced
* What the next step is

---

# Mission

Help individuals, teams, organizations, and educational institutions successfully become AI-Augmented.

---

# Vision

Create a world where people learn to work with AI in ways that enhance human capability, judgment, creativity, and impact.

---

# Core Philosophy

The site is a guide, not a catalog.

Visitors should feel:

> "I know why this matters."
>
> "I understand where I am."
>
> "I know what to do next."

The site should never feel like a collection of products being marketed to visitors.

The site should feel like a movement with a point of view and a practical path forward.

---

# Primary Goal

Move visitors from awareness to action.

Progression:

Aware -> Exploring -> Experimenting -> Integrating -> Leading -> Augmenting

Every page should help visitors advance at least one step along this journey.

---

# Secondary Goals

## Build the Movement

Increase awareness and participation in the AI-Augmented movement.

## Create Community

Help visitors feel that they are part of something larger than themselves.

## Accelerate Learning

Provide practical guidance and resources that help people become AI-Augmented.

## Enable Transformation

Provide pathways for individuals, teams, organizations, and educational institutions to scale AI-Augmented practices.

---

# The Visitor Journey Is The Architecture

The site architecture is not based on products.

The site architecture is not based on content types.

The site architecture is based on transformation.

The journey itself is the primary organizing principle.

---

# The Four Dimensions Of The Journey

## 1. Movement

Every visitor begins here.

Questions:

Why does AI matter?

Why does AI-Augmentation matter?

Why now?

Why should I care?

The purpose of this stage is to create awareness and urgency.

The movement page is the first strategic page after the homepage.

It should establish the public standard, not sell the next product.

---

## 2. Lens

Every visitor views the journey through a specific lens.

The six lenses are:

* Individual
* Team Leader
* Organization Leader
* Student
* Teacher
* Education Administrator

The destination is the same.

The path differs.

Each lens should provide:

* Relevant examples
* Relevant challenges
* Relevant opportunities
* Relevant recommendations

Each lens should also have crawlable stage pages for the AAOS stage model.
The lens landing page can behave like tabs, but each stage should resolve to its own static URL so people, search engines, and AI systems can discover the content directly.

Lens selection is the first guided choice after the homepage.
The site should make it easy for visitors to say "this is me" before asking them to complete an assessment.

---

## 3. AAOS Stages And Augmentation Maturity

Every visitor exists at a different AAOS stage and a different level of augmentation maturity.

The AAOS stage model provides navigation.

Augmentation maturity provides self-awareness.

Suggested stages:

* Aware
* Exploring
* Experimenting
* Integrating
* Leading
* Augmenting

The purpose of augmentation maturity is not scoring.

The purpose is guidance.

Augmentation maturity should be used to reduce ambiguity and guide action.

---

## 4. Learning Path

The site should continuously answer:

What should I do next?

Every visitor should receive a personalized path forward based on:

* Lens
* Maturity
* Goals

The learning path is the practical output of the site.
It should personalize the AAOS experience by lens and augmentation maturity so visitors can explore the framework in the context that fits them.

---

# AAOS Principles

AAOS is the engine behind the AI-Augmented Method.

Visitors should experience outcomes rather than frameworks.

Visitors seek guidance.

The platform uses AAOS to provide:

* Assessments
* Recommendations
* Learning paths
* Transformation roadmaps
* Progress tracking

AAOS should generally remain behind the scenes.

The visitor experiences a journey.

The website should expose outcomes and next steps before it exposes the underlying system.

---

# Content Principles

Content should educate before it promotes.

Content should inspire before it sells.

Content should guide before it persuades.

Content should be practical and actionable.

Every piece of content should answer one or more of the following:

* Why does this matter?
* What does this mean?
* How do I apply this?
* What should I do next?

If a page cannot answer at least one of these questions, it is not ready.

---

# Product Principles

Products are tools.

Products are not destinations.

Books, workshops, courses, assessments, speaking engagements, consulting, and software exist to help visitors advance on their journey.

Products should appear naturally within the journey.

Products should not dominate the experience.

The movement is the frame; the products are supporting paths.

---

# Assessment Principles

Assessments are the primary engagement mechanism.

Assessments help visitors:

* Understand where they are
* Identify gaps
* Discover opportunities
* Receive guidance

Assessments should always produce:

* Current augmentation maturity level
* Strengths
* Improvement opportunities
* Recommended next actions

Assessments should be lens-aware and augmentation-maturity-aware.
They should route visitors into the relevant AAOS exploration path for their lens and augmentation maturity level.

Assessments should lead back into the movement page, the appropriate lens, and the next learning path.

---

# Design Principles

The site should feel:

* Welcoming
* Optimistic
* Practical
* Transformational
* Credible
* Human-centered

Avoid:

* Fear-based messaging
* Excessive hype
* Product-first design
* Consulting-first design
* Technology-centric language

Focus on people, capability, and outcomes.

The visual system should feel calm, informative, and durable.

---

# Navigation Principles

Navigation should be minimal.

Navigation exists to support the journey.

The recommended navigation structure is:

* Home
* The Movement
* Find Your Path
* Resources
* About

The real navigation should occur through:

Lens
 -> Assessment
 -> AAOS exploration
 -> Next Step

The primary onboarding flow should be:

Home
 -> Find Your Path
 -> Assessment
 -> Lens-specific AAOS experience
 -> Resources / Books / Next Step

Not through page hierarchies.

Navigation labels should reinforce the journey, not the internal org chart.

The movement page should be easy to find from every major entry point.

The movement page establishes the public standard, but the site should route visitors into lens-first exploration as quickly as possible.

---

# Success Criteria

The website is successful when visitors can answer:

Why should I become AI-Augmented?

Which lens best describes me?

Where am I today?

What should I do next?

How do I continue progressing?

If visitors can answer these five questions, the site is fulfilling its mission.

The site is also successful when the movement language stays consistent across homepage, movement page, books, resources, and assessment flows.

---

# Final Rule

Whenever a new page, feature, article, assessment, course, product, or design element is proposed, ask:

Does this help someone become AI-Augmented?

If the answer is no, it does not belong on the site.

If the answer is yes, it still has to fit the movement perspective, the canonical journey, and the public launch sequence.
