# AI-Augmented Platform Website Strategy

## Vision, Architecture, and Build Plan

Version: 1.0

---

# Executive Summary

The AI-Augmented website is not a marketing site.

It is not a documentation site.

It is not a training portal.

It is the digital operating system that helps individuals, teams, organizations, educators, and education administrators become AI-Augmented.

The site should guide visitors from awareness to assessment, from assessment to learning, and from learning to implementation.

Every page should help answer one question:

> What is my next step in becoming AI-Augmented?

---

# Mission

Help people become AI-Augmented through the AI-Augmented Operating System (AAOS).

The website should:

* Explain why AI alone is insufficient
* Teach the AI-Augmented mindset
* Help visitors identify their current posture
* Guide visitors through the AAOS journey
* Provide learning resources
* Support implementation
* Enable certification and transformation

---

# Core Principles

## Principle 1: Journey First

The website should guide users through a journey.

Not a collection of pages.

Visitors should always know:

* Where they are
* Why it matters
* What comes next

---

## Principle 2: Lens Driven

Content should be organized around lenses.

Not around documents.

Visitors identify themselves first.

Then receive contextualized content.

---

## Principle 3: Assessment Driven

Assessment becomes the primary conversion mechanism.

The site should constantly answer:

> What is my current posture?

---

## Principle 4: Transformation Focused

The site should focus on outcomes.

Not framework descriptions.

The visitor cares about becoming AI-Augmented.

AAOS is the mechanism.

---

## Principle 5: Free Learning First

The public site teaches the canon.

Revenue is generated through:

* Books
* Workshops
* Training
* Certification
* Consulting
