Diagnosis

Is this really you?

You are past casual use. Now the challenge is consistency, review, and repeatability.

  • You reuse prompts instead of starting from scratch every time.
  • You compare outputs and notice quality differences.
  • You are learning which tasks AI should and should not touch.
  • You still need stronger validation and review habits.
  • You have useful experiments, but not yet a stable personal operating system.

This week

Your mission this week

Choose one workflow and make it dependable instead of merely interesting.

  • Choose one workflow: writing, research, meetings, coding, or content creation.
  • Run the workflow at least three times this week.
  • Document the prompt, the review step, and the failure points.
  • Add one mandatory human validation step before final use.

Practical help

Use these tools and avoid these traps

Recommended tools

  • Advanced Prompt Templates
  • Task-specific GPTs or Assistants
  • Review & Validation checklists

Example Scenario

"Charlie creates a folder of 'Gold Standard' prompts for his recurring monthly reports. He uses these prompts every month and refines them when he encounters errors."

Common mistakes

  • Over-trusting outputs.
  • Running too many disconnected experiments.
  • Failing to document what works.
  • Skipping validation because the answer sounds good.
  • Assuming one prompt fits every task.

AI posture

Be more disciplined about checking, source tracing, and deciding where human review is required.
