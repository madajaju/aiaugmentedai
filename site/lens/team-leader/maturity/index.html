6 Levels of Maturity

The Maturity Ladder

Follow the sequence to build reliable, evidence-backed AI capability.

Level 1

Aware

You see the team problem, but the practice is still inconsistent.

The goal is to make AI use visible and discussable.

Example Scenario

"Sarah, a team lead, sees her team using various AI tools. She starts a weekly meeting to discuss AI but hasn't set any rules yet."

Recommended Tools

  • Team assessments and draft AI policies are useful starting points.
View Level Details

Level 2

Exploring

The team is testing what works.

The goal is to move from individual trial to shared learning.

Example Scenario

"Mike's team pilots AI for their customer support tickets. They learn that AI is fast but sometimes lacks the brand voice, leading them to start a shared 'Brand Voice' prompt library."

Recommended Tools

  • Team prompt libraries and shared use-case logs help the team compare patterns.
View Level Details

Level 3

Experimenting

The team has a few repeatable practices.

The goal is to make quality and review more reliable.

Example Scenario

"Lena's team uses a shared GPT for technical documentation. They notice that the output quality varies by member, so they implement a 'Peer Review Gate' for all AI docs."

Recommended Tools

  • Standardized templates and peer-review prompts support team consistency.
View Level Details

Level 4

Integrating

AI is part of the team workflow.

The goal is to make AI part of a dependable workflow.

Example Scenario

"David's team has a 'Human-in-the-loop' policy for all public-facing content. AI drafts the content, but two humans must sign off on the 'Integrity Packet' before publication."

Recommended Tools

  • Shared quality gates and workflow integration guides matter here.
View Level Details

Level 5

Leading

You are teaching the team how to operate this way.

The goal is to make the standard portable across people and projects.

Example Scenario

"Rachel's team becomes the internal benchmark for AI-augmented design. They publish their 'Validation Ladder' for other teams to use in their own workflows."

Recommended Tools

  • Team playbooks and onboarding modules are central to leadership.
View Level Details

Level 6

Augmenting

The teams operating model is materially different.

The goal is to sustain augmentation without losing human-centered judgment.

Example Scenario

"Tom's team operates as a high-velocity unit where AI handles 80% of routine synthesis. The team's role has shifted to being 'System Architects' who maintain the reliability of the AI-human loop."

Recommended Tools

  • A shared governance stack and continuous audit habits support this stage.
View Level Details

Core Model

The 6 Maturity Levels

The AAOS maturity ladder describes how a person, team, or organization becomes more capable of using AI well over time.

Level General Meaning Diagnostic Signal
Aware Need for AI is visible, but work is mostly manual. Can describe the gap, but not yet close it consistently.
Exploring AI tried in limited cases; learning what helps/fails. Curiosity and first attempts, but no repeatable control.
Experimenting Useful patterns begin to repeat; more deliberate use. Early standardization, but reliability varies by context.
Integrating AI is part of workflow; grounding/validation is routine. Work moves through normal operations without reinvention.
Leading Practice is strong enough to teach or standardize. System can absorb variation while preserving quality.
Augmenting Human expertise and AI are a durable partnership. Reliable enough to improve, transfer, and scale safely.
