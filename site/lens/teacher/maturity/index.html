6 Levels of Maturity

The Maturity Ladder

Follow the sequence to build reliable, evidence-backed AI capability.

Level 1

Aware

You see the classroom pressure, but the response is still forming.

The goal is to connect AI to teaching, learning, and trust.

Example Scenario

"Ms. Adams hears about AI and tries it to draft a permission slip. She sees the potential for saving time but is worried about student cheating."

Recommended Tools

  • MagicSchool AI and policy examples are useful starting tools.
View Level Details

Level 2

Exploring

You are testing how AI can support teaching and feedback.

The goal is to improve the work without weakening the learning.

Example Scenario

"Mr. Bennett uses AI to generate differentiated reading materials for his diverse classroom. He reviews the outputs to ensure the reading levels are appropriate for each group."

Recommended Tools

  • Planning assistants and feedback helpers help you test patterns.
View Level Details

Level 3

Experimenting

AI is beginning to shape your classroom workflow.

The goal is to keep teaching reliable and consistent.

Example Scenario

"Mrs. Chen uses a 'Lesson Plan Engine' she built with custom prompts. She runs every plan through a 'Pedagogical Check' to ensure it meets state standards."

Recommended Tools

  • Templates and feedback prompts support repeatable practice.
View Level Details

Level 4

Integrating

AI is part of how you design and manage learning.

The goal is to make AI part of a dependable workflow.

Example Scenario

"Mr. Diaz has a 'Policy of Transparency' in his syllabus. He uses AI to provide initial feedback on student drafts, but he always provides the final grade and personal commentary."

Recommended Tools

  • Lesson patterns and feedback templates help maintain quality.
View Level Details

Level 5

Leading

You are helping others adopt a better teaching model.

The goal is to make responsible AI use teachable to peers and students.

Example Scenario

"Ms. Evans leads a workshop for her department on 'AI-Resistant Assessments'. She shares her rubric for evaluating how students use AI in their research projects."

Recommended Tools

  • Shared examples and classroom policies support your leadership.
View Level Details

Level 6

Augmenting

AI is changing your teaching operates at a deeper level.

The goal is to sustain augmentation without losing human-centered judgment.

Example Scenario

"Dr. Foster's entire classroom workflow is augmented. He uses AI to track individual student progress in real-time, allowing him to focus on 1-on-1 mentorship and high-level conceptual coaching."

Recommended Tools

  • A stable teaching system and review structure support this stage.

Core Model

The 6 Maturity Levels

The AAOS maturity ladder describes how a person, team, or organization becomes more capable of using AI well over time.

Level General Meaning Diagnostic Signal
Aware Need for AI is visible, but work is mostly manual. Can describe the gap, but not yet close it consistently.
Exploring AI tried in limited cases; learning what helps/fails. Curiosity and first attempts, but no repeatable control.
Experimenting Useful patterns begin to repeat; more deliberate use. Early standardization, but reliability varies by context.
Integrating AI is part of workflow; grounding/validation is routine. Work moves through normal operations without reinvention.
Leading Practice is strong enough to teach or standardize. System can absorb variation while preserving quality.
Augmenting Human expertise and AI are a durable partnership. Reliable enough to improve, transfer, and scale safely.
