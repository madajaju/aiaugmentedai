6 Levels of Maturity

The Maturity Ladder

Follow the sequence to build reliable, evidence-backed AI capability.

Level 1

Aware

The organization knows AI matters, but adoption is uneven.

The goal is to create a common view of the opportunity and the risk.

Example Scenario

"The CEO of GTS announces an 'AI-First' initiative. The company starts tracking which departments are using AI, but policies are still in draft."

Recommended Tools

  • The AI Posture Matrix and three-tier assessments are useful starting tools.
View Level Details

Level 2

Exploring

The organization is comparing use cases and approaches.

The goal is to reduce variance and identify what should scale.

Example Scenario

"An insurance company pilots AI in its claims and marketing departments. They use the 'AI Posture Matrix' to identify which teams are 'AI Fakers' vs 'AI-Augmented'."

Recommended Tools

  • A strategic AI thesis and change management plans support organizational direction.
View Level Details

Level 3

Experimenting

There are repeatable pilots, but not yet a durable model.

The goal is to turn experiments into a managed path.

Example Scenario

"A logistics firm establishes its first 'AI Governance Council'. They require all high-consequence AI pilots to use a standard 'Validation Ladder' for reporting."

Recommended Tools

  • Governance strategies and validation tier ladders support the work.
View Level Details

Level 4

Integrating

AI is now part of the operating model.

The goal is to make AI part of a dependable workflow.

Example Scenario

"A retail giant integrates AI into its supply chain decisions. Every automated decision is backed by an 'Integrity Packet' that can be audited by the risk department."

Recommended Tools

  • Integrity packet flows and integration loops matter at this stage.
View Level Details

Level 5

Leading

The organization is aligning around a shared standard.

The goal is to make adoption durable across functions.

Example Scenario

"A global tech firm standardizes the 'AAOS' across all business units. They use 'Trust Velocity' as a key performance indicator for their digital transformation."

Recommended Tools

  • Trust velocity dashboards and correction load trackers are central.
View Level Details

Level 6

Augmenting

The organization is materially different because of AI.

The goal is to sustain augmentation without losing human-centered judgment.

Example Scenario

"An AI-native startup operates with a completely augmented workforce. Their 'Talent Architecture' is designed around human-AI partnership, allowing them to scale at 10x the industry average."

Recommended Tools

  • A stable governance stack and scale readiness assessments support this stage.
View Level Details

Core Model

The 6 Maturity Levels

The AAOS maturity ladder describes how a person, team, or organization becomes more capable of using AI well over time.

Level General Meaning Diagnostic Signal
Aware Need for AI is visible, but work is mostly manual. Can describe the gap, but not yet close it consistently.
Exploring AI tried in limited cases; learning what helps/fails. Curiosity and first attempts, but no repeatable control.
Experimenting Useful patterns begin to repeat; more deliberate use. Early standardization, but reliability varies by context.
Integrating AI is part of workflow; grounding/validation is routine. Work moves through normal operations without reinvention.
Leading Practice is strong enough to teach or standardize. System can absorb variation while preserving quality.
Augmenting Human expertise and AI are a durable partnership. Reliable enough to improve, transfer, and scale safely.
